Cantina dei Colli Ripani

Cantina dei Colli Ripani is a privately-owned, award-winning cooperative of over 350 growers founded in 1969. Located in Ripatransone (AP), Colli Ripani vineyards cover over 2,000 acres of cultivated vines in Ripatransone and its immediately neighboring communities.

Colli Ripani annually produces over 1 million bottles of wine that include the provincial staple varietals of Rosso Piceno Superiore, Offida Rosso, Marche Rosso, Pecorino, Passerina, Falerio, and Marche Rosato, among others. Its premier wine is a full-bodied red, Khorakhane, a Serbian name meaning, ‘the wind blows through it’. Colli Ripani now also offers a line of biological (organic) wines.

Its wines are offered through the highest quality “Pharus” line, the medium to high quality range called “Colli Ripani”, and a new “Settantase77” line that is marketed specifically to restaurants and hotels.

Collevite: Cantine della Marche

Collevite: Cantine della Marca is a privately-owned, award-winning cooperative comprised of over 25 growers that was founded in 2008. Located in Montsampolo del Tronto (AP), it produces a wide range of wines that includes the provincial staples of Rosso Piceno Superiore, Offida Rosso, Marche Rosso, Pecorino, Falerio, and Marche Rosato as well as Chardonnay, Verdicchio, Cabernet Sauvignon, and Sangiovese, among others. Its premier line is found under the brand name of “Villa Piatti”.

The Collevite winery combines two previous wineries, the Collevite and Villa Piatti wineries. The Villa Piatti winery, located in Ripatransone, was created in 1920 and from that time has been characterized by its high quality wine production.

The cooperative’s lands include acreage in the towns of Carassai, Ripatransone, Castignano, and Offida … the heart of the Rosso Piceno, Rosso Piceno Superiore, and Offida DOCs in the Province of Ascoli Piceno.

Cantine Fontezoppa

Fontezoppa is the name of an ancient spring which flowed the location of the present day vineyards. Everything in Marche has a name rooted in the history and culture of the region: the valleys, ditches, fields and alleys. And it is from the land that we start to tell you about Cantine Fontezoppa.

The Fontezoppa wines present a bold personality, produced on the vineyard’s green hillsides, some facing the sea and others the mountains. The balance between climatic conditions and rich land in Civitanova Alta and Serrapetrona --- both in the Province of Macerata -- is perfect because it is in the area between the Adriatic Sea and the Appenine Mountains that Cantine Fontezoppa grows and ages its best wines.

Sangiovese, Merlot, Cabernet, Lacrima, as well as Maceratino, Incrocio Bruni, and Pecorino are the vine varieties established around Civitanova Alta, where they have the added benefit of the cool sea breezes.

The Vernaccia Nera, Pinot Nero, and Sangiovese vineyards are at the foot of the Appenine Mountains while Serrapetrona is traditionally the land of the Vernaccia, a vine variety with ancient spicy notes that has earned the appreciation of the national wine world and is today at the centre of experimentation and renewed interest.

The secret of a good wine lies in the fraternity of the land combined with an allegiance between the territory and tradition which is the hallmark of Marche varietals. A careful wine waking process which respects tradition combined with modern winemaking processes and aging in the best wooden casks respects the original characteristics of the fruit.

The two vineyards of Cantine Fontezoppa are located in Civitanova (15 ha) and Serrapetrona (20 ha) with annual production of 400,000 bottles.
